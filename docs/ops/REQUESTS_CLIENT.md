# How to respond to a request from your firm

When your firm needs documents or information, they'll send you a checklist inside your Messages portal. This page shows you how to find it, respond, and what to do if a submission gets sent back for changes.

## Finding your requests

You'll see a banner above your Messages list when you have items waiting:

> **You have 3 items to respond to** — Starting with **2024 Tax Documents** → Open

Tap the banner to jump to the conversation, then tap the **Requests** button at the top of the conversation.

Each item shows a colored pill so you can tell at a glance what's needed:

| Pill | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Needed** | Your firm is waiting for this. |
| **Submitted** | You've sent something; your firm hasn't reviewed yet. |
| **Done** | Your firm accepted your response. |
| **Needs more** | Your firm asked for changes. Tap to see their note. |

## Responding to an item

Tap any item that's **Needed** or **Needs more**. You'll see three options:

* **Take photo** — opens your phone's camera. Best for paper documents.
* **Upload file** — pick a PDF, image, or other file from your device.
* **Reply with note** — type a short answer (no file).

You can pick more than one file before sending. Up to **10 files** per submission.

After you tap an option, the panel closes and the message composer at the bottom of the conversation is "pinned" to that item — you'll see a banner like:

> 📌 Replying to **W-2 forms** — attach a file to send

You can:

* Add a sentence ("Here's last year's for comparison") before sending.
* Attach more files using the **Attach** button.
* Tap **Cancel** in the banner if you change your mind.

When you tap **Send**, your message + any attached files go to your firm encrypted. The item flips to **Submitted** within seconds.

## What's encrypted

Everything you upload — file content, message text, the firm's revision notes — is encrypted on your device before it leaves. Your firm decrypts on their device when they review. The list title (e.g. *"2024 Tax Documents"*) is visible to your firm's server so they can send you reminders, but the individual items and your responses are end-to-end encrypted.

## If a submission gets sent back

If your firm asks for changes, the item flips to **Needs more** with their note in red:

> Note from your firm: please re-scan the W-2 — page 2 was cut off

Tap the item again and re-submit. Each submission carries the latest file; your firm sees the full history in the conversation thread.

If your submission was rejected automatically (most often because a file failed virus scanning), you'll see:

> Your last submission couldn't be accepted (possibly an attachment failed virus scan). Please try again.

## Common questions

**Why don't I see a "Submit" button?**
We didn't add a separate submit button — sending the message *is* the submission. The composer's Send button is disabled until you've added enough content to satisfy the item (e.g., a file when the item asks for one).

**Can I respond to multiple items in one message?**
Not directly — each message is linked to one item. The "📌 Replying to" banner shows which one. To answer a different item, tap it from the panel; the previous pin clears automatically when the message is sent.

**Can I see what items are still open across all my engagements?**
Yes — the banner above your Messages list shows the total count and the conversation to start in.

**Do I need an account / password?**
No. You sign in with a code sent to your email or phone (the same flow you used the first time). Each session is short-lived; if you close your browser, you'll need a fresh code.

**What if I uploaded the wrong file?**
The file is encrypted; your firm sees it after they unlock the conversation. Tell them in a follow-up message and they can mark the item back as **Needs more** — your next upload replaces it for review purposes (the original stays in the encrypted history).

## Need help?

Contact your firm directly via the Messages thread. Their staff can:

* Cancel a list you don't need to fill out.
* Mark an item done without you needing to upload anything (e.g. they pulled the document from another source).
* Add or remove items.
