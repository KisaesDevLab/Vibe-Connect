# Files — Client Portal Guide

Your firm's client portal includes a **Files** area for sharing documents
that should live somewhere safer and easier to find than email.

## Where it lives

Sign in to the portal at the URL your firm gave you. After logging in,
you'll see a **Files** link alongside **Messages**.

If you don't see Files, your firm may have it disabled. Ask them to
re-enable it; your existing messages aren't affected.

## What you can do

- **Upload** — pick a document (PDF, image, DOCX, XLSX, CSV, plain text).
  Files are encrypted in your browser before upload; your firm's appliance
  never sees the unencrypted bytes. Files up to 250 MB.
- **Download** — anything your firm has shared with you in the Files area
  is yours to download. The download decrypts in your browser.
- **Delete** — if your firm allows it (most do), you can delete files you
  uploaded yourself. Soft-delete only — your firm can restore. Files your
  firm uploaded for you can only be deleted by them.

## Identity verification

If your firm requires SSN/EIN step-up, you'll be asked to enter the last
four digits of your SSN or EIN before Files opens. The verification lasts
24 hours by default. Failed verification three times in a row revokes your
session and you'll need to log back in.

## What's encrypted

Everything that matters: document contents, filenames, folder names. Your
firm's server stores only ciphertext. The metadata your firm's appliance
*can* see: file type (PDF, JPEG, etc.), size, upload time, and folder
structure. Document contents and names are unreadable without your firm's
keys.

## What we don't put in your inbox

Notification emails about new files never include the filename or any
content — only "you have new files in the portal" with a link. Same for
SMS notifications.

## What if I lose access?

- Lost access to your registered email or phone? Contact your firm — they
  can issue a new access code.
- Suspect someone else got into your account? Contact your firm
  immediately. They can revoke your session and reset.

## What's the difference between Files and chat attachments?

- **Chat attachments** are tied to a specific conversation. Good for
  one-off documents in the flow of a chat.
- **Files** is durable storage that doesn't disappear into a scroll. Good
  for engagement records, signed forms, returns, and source documents you
  want easy to find next year.

Both are encrypted the same way. You can use either; some firms ask
clients to use Files for engagement docs and chat for everything else.
